Open hardware collective debuts a repairable tablet

Topic: Tech

Publication date: 2026-01-22

Source: https://www.ifixit.com/

An open‑hardware collective has unveiled a repairable tablet designed around modular components and long‑term support. The company said the display, battery, and storage are all user‑replaceable with standard tools, while the mainboard can be swapped without adhesives.

Early prototypes are being tested with developer partners, and the group plans to publish full service documentation ahead of a limited production run. The device will ship with an unlocked bootloader and publicly documented drivers.

Executives said the first batch will prioritize education and field‑work use cases, with rugged accessories and a matte display option. Pricing and final specifications are expected later this year.

The collective also committed to multi‑year software updates and a parts store that keeps core modules in stock for at least five years.
