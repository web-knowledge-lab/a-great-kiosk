City heat map guides shade infrastructure

Topic: Climate

Publication date: 2026-01-22

Source: https://climate.nasa.gov/

City officials released a new heat‑risk map that identifies neighborhoods with the highest summer surface temperatures. The data will guide investments in shade structures, street trees, and cooling corridors over the next two budget cycles.

Planners said priority will be given to areas with limited green space and high pedestrian traffic, including school routes and major transit stops. A pilot program will add temporary shade canopies near public markets.

The plan also includes reflective pavement trials, additional public water stations, and expanded outreach to residents who are most vulnerable to heat stress.

Officials said the map will be updated annually and will inform building‑code recommendations for new developments.
